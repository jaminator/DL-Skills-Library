# Term Sheet and IC Templates (P6–P11)

Three templates carry a deal through diligence scoping, the IC decision, and the externalized offer. They are tightly coupled: the IC summary's terms column is a condensed mirror of the term sheet's deal-term sections, and both should be regenerable from a single structured deal-terms source (model + databook + DD).

## Wells & Overland DD List (P6 / P10, .docx)

A joint co-lender diligence request and tracking ledger with an `[Initial]` / `[Follow-Up]` toggle so one shell drives both the P6 Initial DD and P10 Follow-Up DD passes. Structurally it is only a skeleton — a header line plus two numbered sections, "Diligence Questions" and "Data Requests." The substantive request taxonomy is entered per engagement; the template carries no institutional content of its own. Inputs: the target/sponsor identity, the list mode, and the per-deal questions (kick-off requests typically seed it). Outputs: a populated request list issued to the sponsor, whose responses feed the [[dd-analytical-workbooks]], the memos, and the term sheet. Once populated it is MNPI-laden and co-lender-shared — handle as confidential.

## Overland IC Summary (P7 / P11, .pptx)

A single-slide IC scorecard with three tables: (1) a terms grid with columns *Terms | Deal Team Recommendation | Committee Approval / Recommended Changes* over rows Amount & Facility, Commitment, Maturity, Call Protection, Coupon, Amortization, Fees, DDTL Governor, Financial Covenants; (2) a deal/meeting info table (company, leverage, meeting date, meeting type, approval / next steps, deal team); (3) other notes / diligence focus areas. A "meeting type" field switches the same template between the Pre-Screen IC (P7) and Commitment IC (P11). Inputs: the databook and model (facility, commitment %, leverage, coupon, fees, covenant levels), DD findings, and the posting memo's flagged focus areas. It is the live IC-decision capture artifact — and exactly the document the [[ic-and-asset-mgmt-gaps|IC-summary pain point]] describes being built around the meeting from memory. It is **highly RESTRICTED**: it contains IC deliberation content, individual recommended changes, fund economics, and next steps, and must never reach a borrower or co-lender. See [[restricted-content-discipline]].

## Wells & Overland Term Sheet (P8, .docx)

The indicative, non-binding term sheet issued to the borrower, jointly authored by the co-lenders. Sections in order: Proposal Lenders, Borrowers/Guarantors, Collateral, Amount & Facility, Use of Proceeds, Commitment, Maturity, Call Protection, Pricing, Amortization, Fees, Mandatory Prepayments, DDTL Governor, Financial Covenants, Affirmative Covenants, Negative Covenants, Conditions Precedent, plus a leverage-basis footnote and confidentiality disclaimers. Its disclaimer explicitly states it is indicative only and not a commitment, and its conditions precedent reference pending internal committee approvals — confirming it issues after Pre-Screen IC but before final IC approval. Inputs: the model (facility quantums, opening net leverage, pricing grid, ECF step-downs, covenant levels), DD and QoE, the pro forma org review, and pre-screen IC guidance. Its pricing grid and fee economics are RESTRICTED.

## Inputs, outputs, downstream

The cluster consumes the [[dd-analytical-workbooks|DD analytical core]] and [[screening-templates|screening outputs]]. The IC summary's decision drives the IC vote/minutes and advancement; the term sheet's agreed economics flow into the Commitment IC package, EL/Commitment Papers, and ultimately the [[form-credit-agreement|credit and guaranty agreement]] and the [[closing-and-am-templates|closing memo and compliance tracker]] (covenant levels become monitoring inputs). The near-total field overlap between the IC summary and the term sheet is the structural argument for a single deal-terms data product.
