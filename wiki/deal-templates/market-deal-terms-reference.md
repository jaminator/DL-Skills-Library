# Market Deal-Terms Reference

A counsel-prepared (Proskauer Rose, May 2025) market-benchmarking deck comparing middle-market credit-agreement terms across four segments: broadly syndicated, private-credit upper middle market, private-credit middle market, and private-credit middle-market non-sponsored. It is static reference content — no inputs to populate, no automated consumers — used by the deal team and IC to judge whether a proposed deal's terms are "market" when drafting the [[term-sheet-and-ic-templates|term sheet]] and negotiating the [[form-credit-agreement|credit agreement]].

## What it benchmarks

Thirteen topic areas in a four-column segment-comparison format:

- **Financial covenant vs cov-lite** — covenant structures by segment, cov-lite/covenant-loose definitions, typical FCCR (≈1.20–1.00:1.00), minimum liquidity, cash-netting caps.
- **Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA** — the add-back taxonomy: extraordinary/non-recurring, restructuring, cost-savings/synergies caps (≈10–35% shared caps), look-forward periods (12–36 months), "of the type" and QofE language, run-rate pricing.
- **PIK and synthetic PIK** — prevalence, 100–200 bps ranges, sunsets, premiums, synthetic PIK via DDTL.
- **Incremental facilities** — free-and-clear grower baskets (% of EBITDA) and ratio-prong tests.
- **MFN** — scope, 50–75 bps cushions, sunsets, carve-outs.
- **Asset-sale prepayments, Available Amount, ratio baskets, general baskets, unrestricted subsidiaries, non-loan-party investments, voting thresholds**, and **liability-management protections** (J. Crew, Chewy, Serta, Envision, Pluralsight, At Home blockers) by segment.

## Why it matters here

The non-sponsored private-credit middle-market column is the firm's home turf; the deck quantifies how that segment's terms differ from broadly syndicated and sponsored upper-middle-market deals — directly relevant to the [[non-sponsor-friction-premium]]. Its EBITDA add-back taxonomy is the market frame behind the Definitional-vs-Diligence add-back distinction the [[compliance-certificate-parser-pilot|compliance tracker]] enforces, and its covenant-cushion and basket-sizing ranges calibrate the bracketed variables in the [[form-credit-agreement]].

The cov-lite and EBITDA statistics are dated to May 2025 and should be treated as a vintage snapshot; a refreshed deck would supersede the quantitative ranges (a future staleness consideration for [[restricted-content-discipline|content governance]]).
