# Deal Lifecycle Overview

The firm's credit process is an end-to-end workflow spanning **19 phases organized into 6 visible stages**, moving each opportunity from sourcing through portfolio monitoring. The lifecycle is the spine of the underwriting library — every skill, prompt, and project instruction maps to a phase or a transition between phases.

## The six stages

| # | Stage | Phases | What happens |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Origination | P1 Deal Sourcing · P2 NDA Processing | Source candidates via WFSC RMs, sponsors, and external IBs; execute NDA and onboard the deal. |
| 2 | Screening | P3 Kick-Off DDQ · P4 Posting Memo | Issue kick-off DDQ, receive CIM, draft a stoplight-rated posting memo for IC. |
| 3 | Term Sheet | P5 Initial DDQ · P6 Initial DD · P7 Pre-Screen IC · P8 Term Sheet | Run initial DD (databook, model, expert calls), present pre-screen IC, issue term sheet. |
| 4 | Commitment | P9 Follow-Up DDQ · P10 Follow-Up DD · P11 Commitment IC · P12 EL / Commit Papers | Close out residual diligence, secure formal IC commitment, issue exclusivity letter or commitment papers. |
| 5 | Closing | P13 Co-Lender / GP Syndication · P14 Credit Agreement · P15 Closing & Funding · P16 Closing Memo | Syndicate to LPs and select GPs, negotiate the credit agreement, complete KYC and funds flow, hand off to AM. |
| 6 | Asset Management | P17 Portfolio Monitoring · P18 Valuation & LP Reporting · P19 Amendments / Workout | Track compliance certs in Chronograph, mark to market, manage amendments and workouts. |

## Lifecycle economics anchors

- **Conversion.** ~4% lead-to-close (in line with peer BDCs).
- **Throughput.** ~16 closes / year today (4 / qtr at 30 portfolio companies and 8 IPs); ~945–1,220 deals screened per year by 2030 to hit target portfolio.
- **Deal profile.** $25M+ EBITDA, $250–300M facility plus $20–50M+ RCF, non-sponsored, cash-flow oriented.
- **Co-lender base.** 1–6+ LPs and select GPs (BCI, Liberty Mutual, NY Life, National Life, Hamilton Lane, StepStone).

## Why the lifecycle matters here

Every artifact in this library — every skill, prompt, project instruction, schema — maps to a specific phase, a specific team, and a specific deliverable. A skill exists because it compresses one phase's work; a prompt exists because it produces one phase's output; a project instruction exists because one stage's deal context is recurring.

Pain points cluster (see [[pain-point-register]]) in P4 (posting memo), P7 / P11 (IC process gaps), and P17–P19 (asset management). These are the highest-yield targets for the initial library expansion after the pilot. Opportunities (see [[opportunity-register]]) generally take the shape of extract-and-validate (intake-side phases) or generate-with-review (memo and IC phases).
