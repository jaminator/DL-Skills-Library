## Contents

### Financial D&A Section — per-bullet content standards
Six bullets in order, each a single 3–5 sentence paragraph:
1. **M&A / Organic Results** — Isolate organic vs. acquisition-driven growth; quality of headline numbers
2. **Revenue** — LTM, YoY growth, CAGR; price/volume/mix decomposition; concentration flags
3. **Gross Profit** — LTM GP and margin; trend vs. prior periods; pass-through mechanics; range vs. Overland targets (30–40%+)
4. **CA EBITDA & Adjustments** — LTM CA EBITDA and margin; addback bridge; addbacks as % of CA EBITDA; margin vs. own historical baseline
5. **Capex** — LTM capex ($MM and %); maintenance vs. growth split; capex/D&A; FCF conversion
6. **NWC** — NWC balance and ratio; DSO/DIO/DPO; net consumer vs. source of cash; concentration in receivables aging

### Risk Flags — definitions and assignment logic for all 15 items
Y / N / TBD assignment criteria for each flag:
1. **Conc.** (Concentration) — customer/supplier/product/geography thresholds (~10% / sole-source / >30–35% / >50%)
2. **Cyclicality** — historical contraction through downturns; GDP-sensitive end-markets
3. **Seasonality** — quarterly revenue distribution; revolver repayment risk in off-season
4. **NWC Needs** — working capital intensity; DSO/inventory dynamics; revolver dependency
5. **Capex Needs** — maintenance capex vs. revenue thresholds; deferred maintenance signals
6. **Project Based** — discrete project revenue >20–25% of total
7. **Excessive Revolver** — drawn at close; sized >20–25% of funded debt; structural utilization
8. **Bonding / Surety** — surety/performance bond requirements
9. **FX Exposures** — material non-USD revenue/cost exposure (>10%)
10. **Raw Mat Volatility** — commodity input exposure without pass-through
11. **Unique Accounting** — non-standard revenue recognition; complex leases; material addbacks; restatements
12. **Mgmt. Issues** — bench depth, key-man, recent C-suite turnover, governance
13. **Regulatory Risks** — regulated industries, active investigations, compliance exposure
14. **Technology Risks** — displacement/disintermediation risk; platform dependency
15. **ESG** — RR / SA defaults to `n/a` unless user provides

## Financial D&A Section

Six bullets in the order listed below. Each is a single paragraph of 3–5 sentences.
Lead with the key metric. Provide supporting decomposition. Close with the most
important trend or risk signal. Apply the base rate evidence hierarchy (Tier 1/2/3
or internal benchmarking) when flagging sustainability of any metric.

### M&A / Organic Results

Isolate organic vs. acquisition-driven growth for each financial line where the
company has made acquisitions in the trailing period. State the number and type
of acquisitions (tuck-in, platform, etc.), the approximate acquired revenue/EBITDA
contribution, and the implied organic growth rate net of M&A. If no acquisitions
occurred, confirm organic-only performance in one sentence and omit further M&A
commentary. For sponsor-backed credits or recaps, flag whether any pro forma
synergy adjustments are included in the CA EBITDA bridge and assess
their defensibility. This bullet should establish the "quality" of the headline
numbers — are they organic or assembled?

### Revenue

State LTM revenue, the YoY growth rate, and the trailing CAGR if 2+ years of
history are available. Disaggregate growth by its value factors where the source
materials allow: volume (units, jobs, service orders, contracts), price
(rate increases, contractual escalators, mix-up/mix-down), and mix (segment,
channel, or customer composition shifts). Flag if growth is concentrated in a
single factor (e.g., price-led with flat volumes) and assess durability.
For businesses with segment-level disclosure, note the contribution of each
material segment to the revenue total and its growth trajectory. Flag any
revenue concentration by customer (>10% threshold), product, or geography
if disclosed. Do not describe end-market trends without grounding them in the
Company's own data.

### Gross Profit

State LTM gross profit and gross margin. Report gross margin trend vs. prior
periods (2–3 years where available). If gross margin expanded or compressed
materially, identify the driver from source materials — input cost changes,
labor intensity, pricing actions, mix shift, or scale leverage. Note whether
the Company has contractual cost pass-through mechanisms on key inputs (labor
escalators, materials indexing). For labor-intensive businesses, flag the
labor cost as a % of revenue and any disclosed retention/wage dynamics.
For product-based businesses, flag raw material cost exposure and hedge
coverage if disclosed. Flag gross margins below 30% as structurally below
Overland's target range; flag margins materially above 40%+ with a
sustainability note if no structural explanation is provided in source materials.

### CA EBITDA & Adjustments

State LTM CA EBITDA and EBITDA margin. Provide a brief addback bridge: reported
EBITDA → CA EBITDA, with total addback dollar amount and the 2–3 most material
line items. Express total addbacks as a % of CA EBITDA — flag if >15–20% of
CA EBITDA as elevated. Assess each material addback: is it non-recurring (one
specific event, not expected to repeat), truly non-cash, or a management-judgment
item (e.g., run-rate savings, cost-out initiatives, owner compensation
normalization)? Apply heightened skepticism to recurring "non-recurring" items,
owner comp adjustments without market-rate comparators, and forward-looking
synergy claims. Assess LTM EBITDA margin against the Company's own historical
trajectory using the fullest available data. If the margin is above the Company's
own trailing baseline, flag the step-change and its source. Do not characterize
the margin level as "in-line with industry" or "typical for the sector" unless
grounded in Tier 1/2/3 evidence.

### Capex

State LTM capex ($MM and as a % of revenue). Disaggregate maintenance vs. growth
capex where disclosed; if undisclosed, flag. Compare LTM capex to depreciation &
amortization — a capex/D&A ratio below 1.0x may indicate deferred maintenance,
particularly in founder-owned businesses. Note the capex trend vs. prior periods
and whether growth capex is project-specific (i.e., will it continue or step down)?
For asset-light models (<1% capex/revenue), confirm what assets underpin revenue
generation and whether any maintenance investment is being expensed through opex.
State implied FCF conversion (CA EBITDA − maintenance capex) as a % of CA EBITDA
where sufficient data is available.

### NWC

State the NWC balance where disclosed (typically from balance sheet data in the
QoE or CIM financials), the NWC/revenue ratio, and the direction of NWC change
over the available history. Disaggregate into DSO, DIO, and DPO where disclosed;
flag any meaningful deterioration in receivables aging or payables extension.
Identify whether NWC is a net consumer or source of cash as the business grows
(i.e., does incremental revenue require NWC investment?). Flag businesses with
structurally negative NWC (e.g., subscription prepayments, customer deposits) as
a credit-positive. Flag businesses with elongated receivables cycles (DSO >60 days)
or inventory intensity (DIO >45 days) as requiring stress attention. If only a
single balance sheet date is available in the source materials, note this limitation
and report the available snapshot without trend commentary.

## Risk Flags

For each item, apply Y / N / TBD as follows: **Y** if the risk is present and
material; **N** if the risk is absent or definitively immaterial; **TBD** if
insufficient information exists to make a determination from available materials.

Default to **N** only when the source materials provide sufficient evidence to
rule out the risk. Default to **TBD** when material uncertainty exists. Do not
default to **N** simply because the risk is not mentioned in the CIM.

### Conc. (Concentration)

Flag **Y** if any single customer exceeds ~10% of revenue, any single supplier
represents a sole-source or critical single-source dependency, any single product
line or SKU represents >30–35% of revenue, or any single geography represents
>50% of revenue without material diversification. Flag **Y** if the combination
of top-3 customers exceeds ~30–35% in aggregate. The threshold for Y is a
concentration level that would materially impair revenue or operations if lost.
Flag **TBD** if customer concentration is not disclosed.

### Cyclicality

Flag **Y** if the business has demonstrated material revenue or EBITDA contraction
during prior economic downturns (GFC, COVID, sector-specific cycles) visible in
the source financials, OR if the business model has identifiable GDP sensitivity —
discretionary end-markets, construction exposure, industrial/manufacturing
customers, consumer discretionary spend. Flag **N** only if the business is
genuinely acyclical with evidence from historical performance through at least one
recessionary period. Flag **TBD** if the business lacks historical data through
a downturn.

### Seasonality

Flag **Y** if revenue or EBITDA is materially concentrated in one or two fiscal
quarters relative to the annual total, such that a credit drawing on a revolver
in the off-season would face elevated repayment risk. Typical seasonal industries:
landscaping, HVAC, construction, retail, tax services. Flag **N** if revenue is
reasonably distributed across quarters (no quarter >35% of annual revenue without
a credible counterbalancing mechanism). Flag **TBD** if quarterly data is not
available to assess.

### NWC Needs

Flag **Y** if the business requires ongoing working capital funding to support
growth (i.e., incremental revenue consumes NWC), or if NWC is structurally
negative and/or deteriorating in ways that would require revolver draws to fund
operations. A business with high DSO (>60 days), inventory intensity, or rapidly
growing receivables from contract wins is Y. Flag **N** if the business is
asset-light with negative NWC (prepayments, deposits) or stable/improving NWC
metrics. Flag **TBD** if insufficient balance sheet data is available.

### Capex Needs

Flag **Y** if maintenance capex is meaningfully above 3–4% of revenue, if the
business has an ongoing growth capex program that competes for cash alongside
debt service, or if there is evidence of deferred maintenance that creates a
catch-up capex obligation. Flag **N** for genuinely asset-light models where
capex/revenue is consistently below 1–2% and the P&L-expensed maintenance
character of the business is confirmed. Flag **TBD** if capex is not disclosed
or if maintenance vs. growth split is unclear.

### Project Based

Flag **Y** if a material portion of revenue (>20–25%) is derived from discrete
project completions rather than recurring service contracts or reoccurring
maintenance/replacement demand. Project-based revenue is lumpier, harder to
forecast, and more sensitive to economic conditions and discretionary spend.
Common examples: construction, custom manufacturing, one-time implementations,
event-based revenue, and large contract awards. Flag **N** if revenue is
predominantly recurring (contractual) or reoccurring (break-fix, replacement
cycle) with project revenue immaterial. Flag **TBD** if revenue mix is not
disclosed in sufficient detail to assess.

### Excessive Revolver

Flag **Y** if the revolver is expected to be drawn at close for purposes other
than a modest closing adjustment, if the revolver is sized >20–25% of total
funded debt, or if the business model is such that ongoing revolver utilization
is structurally required (seasonal or NWC-intensive businesses). For Overland
purposes, a revolver drawn >0% at close should be noted. Flag **N** if the
revolver is sized as a true liquidity buffer with no expected utilization at
close and no structural NWC/seasonal draw requirement. Flag **TBD** if revolver
sizing and utilization expectations are not disclosed.

### Bonding / Surety

Flag **Y** if the business requires surety bonds, performance bonds, or similar
financial assurance mechanisms as a condition of winning or executing contracts —
common in construction, government contracting, environmental services, and
utilities. The presence of bonding requirements can limit the borrower's financial
flexibility, create contingent liabilities, and complicate a lender's ability to
take control in a restructuring. Flag **N** if bonding is explicitly absent or
immaterial to the business model. Flag **TBD** if not addressed in source materials.

### FX Exposures

Flag **Y** if the business has material revenue, costs, or balance sheet items
denominated in non-USD currencies that are not fully hedged. "Material" is
approximately >10% of revenue or costs in a foreign currency. Flag **N** if the
business is entirely USD-denominated or if foreign currency exposure is de minimis
and unhedged. Flag **TBD** if geographic revenue mix is not disclosed with
sufficient currency-level detail.

### Raw Mat Volatility

Flag **Y** if the business has a key input cost exposed to commodity price
volatility (energy, metals, resins, agricultural commodities, lumber, etc.) and
lacks contractual pass-through mechanisms that fully insulate gross margin.
Flag **N** if the business is a pure services model with no material commodity
input, or if comprehensive contractual pass-through is in place and validated
in the source materials. Flag **TBD** if input cost structure is not disclosed
in sufficient detail to assess.

### Unique Accounting

Flag **Y** if the business uses non-standard revenue recognition policies (e.g.,
percentage-of-completion, bill-and-hold, variable consideration under ASC 606),
has complex lease accounting with material operating lease obligations that
function economically like debt, has unusual EBITDA addbacks that require
specialized accounting knowledge to evaluate (e.g., lease normalization,
fair value adjustments), or has had auditor changes, restatements, or qualified
audit opinions. Also flag Y for businesses with material deferred revenue that
creates timing differences between billings and GAAP revenue. Flag **N** if
accounting is straightforward and no unusual policies are evident. Flag **TBD**
if financial statements are not available at the time of posting.

### Mgmt. Issues

Flag **Y** if there is evidence of thin management bench depth, heavy key-man
concentration in a single individual (typically the founder/owner), recent
C-suite turnover (especially CFO or COO within 12–18 months), or governance
concerns (e.g., family conflict, management team misalignment with lender
interests in a stress scenario). For sponsor-backed credits, flag Y if the
management team has recently been installed by the sponsor without a track
record at the business. For non-sponsored founder-owned credits, apply higher
scrutiny — founder dependency is common and should be assessed against the
breadth of the management bench below. Flag **N** only when the management team
has clear depth and succession. Flag **TBD** when management depth is not
addressed in source materials.

### Regulatory Risks

Flag **Y** if the business operates in a regulated industry where changes in
licensing requirements, rate setting, environmental compliance, healthcare
reimbursement, or financial services regulation could materially impair revenue
or operating costs. Also flag Y for businesses with active regulatory
investigations, consent decrees, or pending material compliance expenditures.
Common regulated industries: healthcare, financial services, environmental
services, utilities, childcare, education, cannabis. Flag **N** if the business
operates in an unregulated or lightly regulated industry with no identified
regulatory exposure. Flag **TBD** if regulatory environment is not discussed in
available materials.

### Technology Risks

Flag **Y** if the business faces credible near-to-medium-term risk of
technological displacement or disintermediation — AI, automation, platform
aggregation, or competitive technology that could render the business model
obsolete or compress pricing power. Also flag Y if the business is heavily
dependent on a single technology platform, software system, or IT
infrastructure without disclosed redundancy. Flag **N** if the business model
is inherently resistant to technological displacement (e.g., hands-on trade
services, physical infrastructure maintenance, human-judgment-intensive work)
or if the competitive technology risk is de minimis given the Company's
end-market. Flag **TBD** if the technology environment is not addressed in
source materials.

### ESG

Default to **n/a** unless the user explicitly provides ESG Risk Rating (RR)
and Structural Accommodation (SA) values. Do not attempt to derive ESG ratings
from source materials. Do not mark as TBD — mark as **n/a**.
