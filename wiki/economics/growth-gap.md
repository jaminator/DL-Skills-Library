# The Growth Gap

The Growth Gap is the difference between the firm's status-quo trajectory and the diversification target needed to reach BDC peer scale by year-end 2030.

## The numbers

| Metric (YE 2030) | Status Quo | Growth Target | Gap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portfolio companies | 110 | 225 | +115 names |
| Cumulative funded AUM | $8.3B | $16.9B | +$8.6B |
| NAV | $3.9B | $8.0B | +$4.1B |
| Total annualized fee revenue | $120M | $245M | +$125M / yr |
| 5-year cumulative fee revenue | $425M | $710M | +$285M |

At today's pace (~16 closes / yr), the firm reaches ~110 portfolio companies by 2030. The growth target needs ~47 closes / yr by 2030 — roughly a **3× lift in qualified lead volume**.

## The binding constraint

**Supply, not conversion.** The firm already converts at the peer-benchmark 4% lead-to-close rate. The throughput problem is upstream — qualified lead volume needs to ramp roughly 3–4× to support 200–250 portfolio names by 2030.

The lead-enhancement lever is **P1 sourcing automation**. Proprietary screens on NAICS Association, PitchBook, Preqin, and CapIQ help WF mine non-sponsored flow from its relationship network. This is a step-change in lead enhancement, not a parallel lead-generation function.

## The diversification target

Leading BDC peers carry 200–250 portfolio companies; the firm today has ~30. The target is institutional-quality diversification consistent with the leading BDC cohort — high enough name count to absorb single-credit losses without distorting fund returns.

| 2027 | +14 | +1,050 | +500 | +17.4 |
| 2028 | +29 | +2,175 | +1,035 | +49.0 |

| **5-yr** | **+115** | **+8,625** | **+4,106** | **+$285M cumulative** |

The fee delta is back-loaded — the 2028–2030 years carry most of the value because the close ramp compounds.

## What automation does to the gap

Automation is the **enabling condition** for the ramp. P1 lead enhancement is the lever; downstream automation across P3 → P19 is what makes the implied throughput **executable** with the headcount the firm is willing to carry. Without the [[efficiency-dividend]] reducing labor per name, hitting 225 names would force ~120 IPs at the peer-average 1.88× cos/IP — see [[non-sponsor-friction-premium]].

The Growth Gap and the Efficiency Dividend are independent ROI levers but mutually reinforcing: closing the Growth Gap requires the productivity uplift the Efficiency Dividend produces, and the Efficiency Dividend amortizes itself over a larger book.

## Load-bearing assumption

The ramp embeds a +2.44× lift over today's 4% lead-to-close baseline. P1 ships H2 2026; uplift is currently unmeasured — the deck flags this as a re-mark-required assumption after 6 months of production data.
